Key Notes

Smoking:

Alcohol consumption was associated with an increased risk for lung cancer among men consuming at least 21 drinks per week.

Smoking + Drinking:

"Dr. Lynch says. "There's a much more exponential synergy when you combine alcohol and smoking." People who drink and smoke are 15 times more likely to develop cancer in the mouth and throat than non-drinkers and non-smokers, according to the NIH.

Dysphagia:

Many patients with lung cancer experience difficulty in swallowing. The medical term for this symptom is dysphagia and refers to any dysfunction in swallowing. Dysphagia can occur due to a number of reasons, including the direct impact of tumors, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and targeted therapies such as EGFR (epidermal growth factor receptor) inhibitors.

Yellow finger:

The skin can be affected by lung cancer that has spread, different syndromes associated with lung cancer, and treatments for lung cancer. Yellow skin, itchy skin, easy bruising, swelling, and changes in facial sweating may be seen. If lung cancer has spread to the pancreas or liver, it may cause jaundice. Jaundice is when your skin or the whites of your eyes turn yellow. It is caused by a build-up of bilirubin in the body.

Fatigue:

A low red blood cell count, or anemia, is a very common effect of lung cancer. Anemia can make you really tired because your body's tissues aren't getting enough 14 oxygen. And generally speaking, cancer cells like to feed off the nutrients you need to power through a day.

Chronic diseases:

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is a significant risk factor for lung cancer. Approximately 1% of COPD patients develop lung cancer every year, which may be associated with genetic susceptibility to cigarette smoke.

Chronic + Difficult is breathing:

Asthma is a chronic condition of the lungs that causes narrowing and inflammation of the airways. It also causes an increase in mucus production in the air passages, which may decrease airflow while breathing. This especially affects breathing out, according to a 2021 research article.

Peer Pressure:

The current aggregated meta-analysis revealed that peer pressure had a significant influence on cigarette smoking: OR: 2.68(95% CI:2.37,3.03) by ncbi.nlm.nih.gov

Hemoptysis:

Lung cancer is responsible for approximately 23% of all cases of hemoptysis. The highest incidence of bleeding occurs in patients with squamous cell histology, and massive hemoptysis was associated with both cavitation and tumour arising from or eroding through the central airways.
